Water has a high heat capacity, which is why the temperature at which it boils (100 degrees Celsius, 212 degrees Fahrenheit, or 373 degrees Kelvin) is so high! The high heat capacity of water is very useful, because this way water can absorb temperatures without flaming up, which would be bad for humans, because 72% of the human body--is water! Water is the only substance on Earth commonly found in all 3 states--and solids, ice, is one of these states. Water freezes at 0 degrees Celsius, 32 degrees Fahrenheit, and 273 degrees Kelvin. Water is a polar molecule, so ice floats on water, which is something that doesn't happen with most other substances! Water is made up of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom. To form a water molecule, an oxygen atom, which behaves as if it need 2 more electrons, finds the said two electrons in two hydrogen atoms. This forms a water molecule. In the nucleus of the oxygen atom, there are 8 protons and 8 neutrons. However, the two hydrogen atoms have one proton each in their nuclei, so altogether a water molecule has 10 electrons, 10 protons, and 8 neutrons. Water exists in three states: solid, liquid, and gas. The solid form is ice. The liquid form is water. The gaseous form is water vapor. Water is the only substance on Earth commonly found in all 3 states on Earth! Water shows surface tension, adhesion, and cohesion. 71% of Earth is all water! Only 29% of Earth is land. 97% of the water on Earth is salt water, and of the remaining 3%, 2% is frozen in glaciers and other iceforms. The last 1% is drinkable fresh water. However, luckily for us, water cycles forever, so that we always have the same amount of water on Earth. The pH scale is a measure of how acidic or basic something is. Water is a good, neutral seven.
