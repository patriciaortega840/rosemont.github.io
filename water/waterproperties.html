THE PROPERTIES OF WATER



There are many cool properties of water, many of which were mentioned on the page before this one. However, this page explains everything more thoroughly.

The most important property?

ALL of the properties of water are incredibly important! Without any of them, life wouldn't
be possible! Amazing, isn't it? The properties are pretty much codependent on each other, and
we're codependent on all of them! Now, we'll learn about the properties.

One of the most important properties is water's polar molecularity!

Sounds odd, doesn't it? But those odd sounding words are incredibly important to life on Earth. It's
water's polar molecularity that means ice can float on water. It's what causes water to be such a great
solvent (which is important: our bodies need to dissolve things in our blood!). It's why life can
survive on Earth.

You see, if water was no such an interesting oddball of a molecule, ice couldn't float on water. And it's not a stretch to say that if ice couldn't float on water, life on Earth couldn't exist.

Some more important properties are: 1. The pH of water is 7 (if it were more basic, or more acidic, we wouldn't be able to drink it!)
2. At 4 degrees Celsius, water's density is exactly one gram per milliliter.
3. It can be a solid, liquid, or gas!
4. It's a universal solvent, which means it dissolves things really well! If it weren't such a great solvent, then our blood could not dissolve the various things that pass through it, which would be very problematic indeed.
5. It's cohesive, adhesive, and shows surface tension. That's why water striders can lay on water.
6. It freezes at 0 degrees Celsius, 32 degrees Fahrenheit, and 273 degrees Kelvin.
7. It boils at 100 degrees Celsius, 212 degrees Fahrenheit, and 373 degrees Kelvin.
8. It is clear and odorless!
9. Water molecules have two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom (thus, H2O)
10. It has a high heat capacity!
